Product Design · Case Study

Interactive Oncology Reporting

Transforming a static, 20-page PDF into a real-time results portal — giving oncologists the right genomic insights, at the right time, to make better treatment decisions for their patients.

Foundation Medicine
Lead Product Designer
IDEO
Web · Mobile

The Challenge

Communicating complex genetic information to multiple users

Foundation Medicine's Comprehensive Genomic Profiling (comprehensive genomic profiling) tests generate rich, complex data about a cancer patient's unique genetic signatures. But delivering those insights through a dense, static PDF report was creating friction for the oncologists, pathologists, patients, and caregivers who needed to act on them.

How might we deliver a superior customer experience and transformative patient empowerment through an easy-to-use, integrated, dynamic digital reporting experience — one that ensures patients get the optimal treatment?

"How might we deliver a dynamic digital reporting experience that ensures patients get the optimal treatment?"
1

Identify opportunities to communicate effectively and succinctly with oncologists about their patients' comprehensive genomic profiling results.

2

Leverage interactivity and real-time information sharing to inform oncologists quickly, using the channels that they prefer.

3

Understand what information oncologists want to see first, next, and last as they interpret comprehensive genomic profiling test results.

Research

Understanding oncologists and the context of genomic testing

The initiative began with a deep dive into how oncologists work — their varying levels of familiarity with genomic testing, the clinical decisions they need to make, and what information they need most at the moment of reading a report. This included in-depth interviews, contextual inquiry, and analysis of how the current reports were (and weren't) meeting their needs.

The Foundation Medicine Design Team then partnered with IDEO to research patients and develop archetypes for patients and their caregivers. This research helped us understand patients' needs for interpreting their genomic testing results, how those results can and should be communicated, and in what ways Foundation Medicine can serve as an ally in their cancer journey.

Problems + opportunities

Problems

  1. 1.Oncologists are spending their time fighting insurance companies
  2. 2.Oncologists don't trust their EMRs
  3. 3.Oncologists are overwhelmed with information overload
  4. 4.Oncologists don't have time to read full reports
  5. 5.Oncologists have limited time to do research into new treatments
  6. 6.Oncologists have superficial understanding of some treatments
  7. 7.Oncologists who are clinically focused and who are research focused are in both community and academic settings

Opportunities

  1. 1.How might we…arm oncologists with the right information/documentation to streamline their discussions with insurance companies?
  2. 2.How might we…establish trust and credibility when integrating the ability to order our test into local EMRs?
  3. 3.How might we…direct oncologists to the information that they need to take action?
  4. 4.How might we…simplify and condense important findings so that oncologists are able to identify them and take action?
  5. 5.How might we…provide access to research and findings to allow oncologists to capitalize on their limited amount of time?
  6. 6.How might we…establish a deeper understanding and familiarity with treatment options on our reports?
  7. 7.How might we…segment oncologists more accurately to represent their motivations and behaviors and not just their locations?

Key Insight

Three questions oncologists always bring to a report

💊

Is the current therapy working?

Oncologists want to quickly validate whether a patient's current treatment aligns with their latest genomic findings.

🧬

Any new biomarkers to pay attention to?

New or changed genomic signatures can signal a need to adjust the treatment pathway.

🔬

Are there relevant clinical trials nearby?

Access to relevant trials close to the patient's location is a critical, time-sensitive need.

Design

From static PDF to interactive results portal

Based on the report taxonomy analysis, user needs research, and insight into how clinicians use — and want to use — genomic data, the design team began iterating on an interactive reporting experience. The vision: transform the Foundation Medicine report from a static, 20+ page PDF into a real-time results portal where oncologists could dig as deeply as they needed into relevant clinical data for their patients.

The design prioritized three critical elements: prioritized genetic findings from comprehensive genomic profiling testing, FDA-approved therapies and precision medicines, and access to relevant clinical trials — each accessible via progressive disclosure on both mobile and desktop formats.

Pilot & Outcomes

Testing, learning, and refining

The design team worked closely with translational oncology, product strategy, marketing, and engineering to pilot the interactive report with a select group of Foundation Medicine customers. Feedback confirmed the value of the interactive format for on-the-go results review, while also surfacing an important nuance: the PDF still had utility for clinicians who rely on the appendix content for detailed gene and biomarker information, and for copying findings into patient EMR progress notes.

Feedback

"The findings could be organized from most relevant to least relevant. BRCA2 is most relevant in this case, and AR is less so. Perhaps this could be pushed down."
"For the clinical trials, it would be nice to have the distance, like within 250 miles of the zip code of my practice."
"I would love to see more information on relevant clinical trials — which ones are open and enrolling, the trial names, where they're located, and even have a link to them for more information."
"I like the recommended therapies section – with the NCCN recommendations here, it makes it easier to select a therapy."
"When it comes to findings, I want to know what gene or genes I can act on now. So show me what's actionable there."
"Therapies and trials pages are very clear to me, and I love how I can drill down to get more information on the therapies, specifically. This feels like the right amount of information for me here."
"All this stuff on the findings tab is taking up too much real estate for me – I don't want to have to scroll to look at all of the mutations."
"This back button confuses me – I would assume I'd be going back to all of the reports for this patient."
"I might want to still download the PDF report, but this doesn't need to be front and center on the interactive report."
"Could there be a summary page, with findings, therapies, and trials all in one blurb?"

Subsequent refinements focused on: surfacing the most actionable genetic results first, displaying patient progress over time through a new monitoring feature, and simplifying key clinical insights to make oncologist review faster and more confident.
