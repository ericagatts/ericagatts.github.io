Medicare Differentiation

A research-led innovation initiative to design differentiated Medicare experiences for the 60 million Americans aging into the program — resulting in a personalized digital tool that increased pre-Medicare member engagement by 15%.

The Problem

60 million Americans aging into Medicare — and a crowded market to compete in

With over 60 million Americans enrolling in Medicare every year, Highmark's healthcare offerings for seniors face strong competition. To stay competitive, Highmark needed to create differentiated experiences, services, and solutions for people aging into Medicare in the next 5–10 years.

The initiative was tasked with understanding this "age-in" population deeply — their needs, anxieties, and expectations — and designing a product concept that would set Highmark apart.

Research

Understanding the needs and behaviors of people aging into Medicare

My team conducted field research and stakeholder interviews to build foundational insights on the age-in population across four areas of their lives. We then leveraged market research from the Blue Cross Blue Shield Association to test and support our qualitative findings — and to develop four key personas that represented the range of age-in needs and motivations.

🏥

Health Needs

Managing chronic conditions, understanding coverage options, navigating the complexity of Medicare parts A, B, C, and D.

💰

Financial Concerns

Balancing fixed income against rising healthcare costs — and finding plans that deliver real value without surprises.

🤝

Support Systems

Many age-ins rely on family members and caregivers when making Medicare decisions — the experience needed to serve them too.

📱

Digital Comfort

Varying levels of digital comfort shaped how concepts needed to be designed — simple, guided, and trustworthy above all.

Key Insight

It wasn't about the perks — it was about the plan

The first round of concept testing validated the core idea, but also surfaced a critical insight: participants felt the most important aspect of the experience was the personalized Medicare plan guidance, not the à la carte wellness perks. Age-ins wanted the tool to surface Medicare plans and services matched to their specific needs — not a menu of add-ons.

This reframe guided the final design: a "smart" questionnaire that leverages a patient's existing EMR and insurance data alongside their quiz responses to deliver a personalized report outlining the best Medicare plans, services, and coverage for that individual.

"What matters most to us is knowing which plan is right for our situation — not all the extra perks in those plan packages."
— Age-in participant, concept testing

Outcomes

Launched as a Highmark product in Q3 2021

After delivering the finalized concept to partners in Senior Markets, Highmark's digital team built and launched a pilot with the design team's guidance. Member feedback was largely positive, and the tool was refined and released as a full Highmark product.
