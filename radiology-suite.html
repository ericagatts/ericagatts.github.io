The Work

Protocoling: a critical workflow that had never been digitized

Protocoling is the process by which radiologists review a patient's clinical information and create specific instructions — or protocols — for how an imaging exam should be performed. It is one of the most essential ways radiologists ensure best possible care for patients and prevent unnecessary imaging and radiation exposure.

Until 2015, this workflow had never been available in GE's radiologist-facing applications. Radiologists had to log into multiple hospital systems, review a patient's scheduled exams, and then use paper forms to complete the protocoling step. My role was to bring this into GE's Workflow Manager application — end to end, from discovery through pilot.

Research

Multi-stakeholder research across complex clinical workflows

I conducted in-depth interviews and contextual inquiries with radiologists, technologists, and clinical staff across UPMC and New York Presbyterian Hospital to understand how protocoling actually works — the tools used, the decisions made, the friction points, and the stakes involved. Protocoling directly influences which imaging exam is performed and can shape health outcomes and critical diagnoses.

Fragmented Context

Radiologists logged into multiple hospital systems to gather the clinical context they needed before protocoling.

📝

Paper-Based Protocols

The actual protocol was written on paper forms — disconnected from the digital systems tracking the patient's exam.

⏱️

Time-Critical Decisions

Protocoling delays affect scheduling and patient throughput across the entire radiology department.

👥

Multi-Stakeholder Workflow

Radiologists, technologists, and ordering physicians each play a role — and need visibility into the same protocol.

Design & Testing

Iterating through two rounds of testing

I developed conceptual designs for protocoling in Workflow Manager and tested them with radiologists at UPMC and stakeholders at New York Presbyterian. The designs were well received — particularly the ability to build worklists for exams to be protocoled, access in-app clinical context, and search, edit, and comment on protocols without leaving the diagnostic application.

After protocoling was prioritized for development in summer 2016, I ran a second round of testing on refined designs. Radiologists responded positively to the core workflow — but flagged that a set of status icons (recommended by product management for parity with a legacy GE app) was confusing and visually noisy. Those were removed and replaced with a simpler, cleaner approach in the final implementation designs.

Outcome

From paper forms to native radiology workflow

The final designs — incorporating iterative feedback from two rounds of testing — were handed off to engineering and entered development. The feature was piloted across multiple radiology departments and tested for regulatory compliance, marking the first time a native protocoling workflow had been available in GE's radiologist-facing application suite.
